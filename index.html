Letter To Members

Aloha, fellow MBCAII members!

A group of us -- long-term residents, new property owners, and former board members -- urge you to pay special attention to this year's annual election. Many of us have lived here full time for many years and may have a perspective that you find valuable.

We appreciate the work the board outlined in their mailer dated 2/15/18. A person who gives of their time and energy without compensation deserves appreciation. We thank those that have served.

What the board letter does not reveal is that the board received a legal opinion dated 1/17/18 that invalidated the elections they held for the last 4 years. Proxies have been malformed continuously. We have included this legal opinion that the board received, but failed to share with its membership. There are multiple possibilities, some intentional and some not, why a change was made to the proxy in 2014. The result of the change enhanced the board's significant power to determine the results of the election of directors and the assessment of dues.

Distant owners may not grasp the culture of the management and the present board. There is a list of fine people who feel that they have been either pushed out, forced out, or chose to leave their board seat because their opinions were not always in harmony with management and the board members who regularly agree with management.

Paul Cox is one of these people. Paul was appointed to a vacant position. His position was confirmed by membership in the elections held after his appointment. Paul has devoted himself to understanding the laws of Hawaii as they relate to homeowners associations. Through his efforts, he brought notice of the malformed proxies to the HOA board. The result? He was told his position on the board was invalid, and he was no longer seated. Since then, two other people have been appointed to the board. How is that possible?

We appreciate Paul for identifying this liability. His efforts protect us all. He should be elevated on the board...not removed. Take a moment to consider that a legally invalid board has filed liens, foreclosed on property and authorized significant expenses.

The letter also does not make mention of the extensive use of “executive sessions” by the current board. “Executive sessions” are meetings in secret without public minutes. We have learned that the last 18 months of meetings have all been in executive sessions. This lack of transparency is troubling to us and should be to you. Do you realize that the board just created rules that affect every owner and every property without any open conversation with the membership? They met in “executive session” without public comment or published minutes of their meetings. This is unacceptable.

The board letter dated 2/15/18 asks you to “Fill out your proxy naming the Majority of the Board”. Please don't. Because the majority of proxies are assigned to the board, the board has been static and self-perpetuating. It has been virtually impossible to be seated on the board without being selected by the board itself. We need your help to increase the diversity of opinion on the board.

We ask instead that you work with us to elect a board that has a clear commitment to transparency through open meetings, works to engage the membership in decision making, respects and welcomes different opinions and keeps membership safer by cautious adherence to legal election procedures.

Because you cannot vote without being present, we ask that you give your proxy to one of the candidates listed below.

  • Paul Cox
  • Armando Cruz
  • Lynne Houston
  • Kathy Kuharski
  • Pam McBride
  • Oscar Salgado
  • Dr. Patricia Mullen

If you do not want to support these candidates individually, at a minimum, please check the proxy box that says, "For quorum purposes only". This will allow the meeting to be held, all voices to be heard, and all votes to be counted fairly.

What Can You Do?

Do not give the current board a blank check to continue doing business as usual. If you cannot attend the annual meeting, give your proxy vote to one of the highly-qualified individuals committed to creating a more inclusive and open Board.

If you need a proxy or have already sent in your proxy and would like to change your vote you can download and print one. Because a date is furnished in the proxy, the most recent proxy will be the one counted.
