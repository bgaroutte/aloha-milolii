The Issues in Detail

MBCA II Elections Were Invalid

Feb 17 2018 by Paul Cox

It has come to light that all but 1 of the elections held in the last 10 years were illegal.

According to the Association's own attorney who reviewed the Board's practices against State Law:

...the proxy forms that were sent out to the membership for their voting in the years 2014, 2015, 2016 and 2017 inadvertantly failed to include the box for members of the Association to give their proxy to the members of the Board of Directors present at the meeting "with the vote to be shared with each board member receiving an equal percentage"

...The statutes are very clear that for a proxy to be valid it must comply with the statuatory requirements. If it does not, it is an invalid proxy. All of the proxies returned for use in the 2014-2017 elections failed, even if inadvertently, to include all of the statuatory requirements, therefore all proxies utilized for those elections are deemed invalid.

What Can You Do?

Do not give the current board a blank check to continue doing business as usual. If you cannot attend the annual meeting, give your proxy vote to one of the highly-qualified individuals committed to creating a more inclusive and open Board.
